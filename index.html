School closure / consolidation transparency

The future of our children and communities in the Morongo Basin depends on strong, accessible public schools. Recent discussions about closing as many as four elementary schools as early as Fall 2027 have raised serious concerns among parents, staff, and residents. The neighborhood schools currently under discussion — Morongo Valley Elementary School, Landers Elementary School, Friendly Hills Elementary School, and Palm Vista Elementary School. They are essential parts of our communities. They provide children with stability, support, and access to education close to home. They also serve families and neighborhoods in ways that cannot be easily replaced once a school is closed. These decisions will affect every school community in the district — not only schools that may close, but also “receiving schools” that could face larger class sizes, boundary changes, transportation impacts, staffing shifts, and program changes. Before any closure moves forward, families and residents deserve the full picture: real site-by-site savings, transition costs, transportation impacts, alternatives to closure, administrative reduction options, and a clear fiscal stabilization plan. School closures are not just budget decisions. They can reshape neighborhoods, families, and communities for decades.
